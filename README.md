**Making the Most of Your Subscription**

Assuming you've decided AdTargeting makes sense for your advertising strategy, there are some approaches that maximize what you get out of the platform.

Start with broad interest research even if you think you already know your audience. Many advertisers discover that their assumptions about their target market don't fully align with the data. Search for obvious interests related to your product, then explore the related suggestions that AdTargeting surfaces. Some of your best-performing interests might be two or three clicks away from where you started.

Use the audience comparison feature religiously before committing to interests in your campaigns. Just because two interests sound similar doesn't mean they'll perform the same way. The demographic differences can be significant, and knowing whether one skews older, more educated, or more geographically concentrated helps you choose the better fit for your specific offer.

When researching competitors with the ad spy tool, focus less on copying their creative and more on understanding their strategy evolution. Look at how their messaging has changed over time, which offers they've tested, and where they seem to be allocating budget across platforms. This gives you insights into what worked and what didn't without having to spend your own money testing.

Build comprehensive interest packages for different campaign types rather than researching interests ad hoc every time you launch something new. If you're running lead generation campaigns, create a package of interests that historically drive high-quality leads. For product sales, build a separate package optimized for conversion intent. Having these ready to deploy means you can launch campaigns faster and maintain consistency in your targeting approach.

Don't ignore the geographic and demographic filters when searching interests. An interest that performs well in the United States might have completely different characteristics in Canada, the UK, or Australia. Understanding these regional differences helps if you're expanding into new markets or need to adjust messaging for different countries.

Set aside time monthly to explore new interests and check competitor activity even if your current campaigns are performing well. Markets shift, new interests gain traction, and competitors launch new campaigns. Staying current with these changes keeps your targeting fresh and helps you spot opportunities before they become obvious to everyone else.

Export your best-performing interest data and cross-reference it with your Facebook Ads Manager results. Over time, you'll develop intuition for which interest characteristics correlate with campaign success in your specific niche. This makes your future targeting decisions faster and more accurate.
